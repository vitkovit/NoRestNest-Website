What it does

Everything you need at the rack.

No social feed, no ads, no fluff. Open it, log your set, get back to lifting.

Progressive overload, precalculated

You walk in. Your next set's weight is already decided. Last one felt easy, it goes up. You missed reps, it backs off. You stop being your own program-design committee at 6am.

Logging takes longer to read about than to do

Two taps per set. The rest timer runs itself. Reshuffle the order mid-workout without losing the working weight. The app gets out of the way.

Squat, push-up, plank — it knows the difference

Barbell, bodyweight, and timed work each progress on their own scale. Tune how aggressive that is, or trust the defaults.

Hit your macros without doing math

Snap your plate, scan a barcode, or just type the food. Calories, protein, carbs, fat — logged. Water alongside, meal-prep batches if you cook ahead, and today's targets always showing what you still owe yourself.

Your body, mapped to what you actually did

What's fried, what's recovered, what's been ignored for three weeks. Ends the "did I hit shoulders this week?" debate.

Charts that go up when you do

PRs, weekly volume, bodyweight — one screen, no spin. They go up when you show up. They don't when you don't. Either way, they don't lie.

Every screen, doing its job.

Log a set, hit your macros, see where your body is going. No demo mode. No stock photos. The real thing.

What it isn't

Things NoRestNest won't do.

Because there are already too many apps doing them.

It won't pretend to be your coach.

The math suggests the next weight. That's it. No motivational quote, no fake voice in your head, no "you got this!" notification at 6am.

It won't gamify your gains.

No XP. No levels. No badges for "Squat Apprentice — Tier 2." You're not collecting points. You're collecting muscle.

It won't sell your data. To anyone.

No ad SDKs, no analytics broker, no "anonymized aggregate" cop-out. Your protein intake is not a product.
